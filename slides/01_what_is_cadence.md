## What Is Cadence?

Cadence is an AI-powered weekly operational review platform built for executives, founders, and senior leaders who want to operate at the highest level — without the highest price tag.

Every week, Cadence sits down with you.

It reviews your priorities, your task completion, your deep work, your friction patterns, and your execution score. It then draws on the frameworks of the world's most respected leadership thinkers to tell you exactly what's happening, why, and what to do about it.

It is part accountability partner, part performance analyst, part executive coach.

## What Makes Cadence Different

Most productivity tools measure activity.  
Cadence measures **execution quality**.

Every week you complete a structured session that captures:

- Your 3 priorities and whether they were done, partial, or deferred
- 10 tasks with completion status
- Deep work hours
- Primary friction type
- Execution score out of 10
- Your honest reflection

Cadence then generates an AI coaching report grounded in the knowledge base — not generic advice, but specific frameworks from specific authors applied to your specific week.

> *"Your 7.2 hours of deep work is structurally insufficient for a CEO whose primary friction is meetings — grounded in Cal Newport's fixed-schedule productivity framework."*

Cadence learns from the same thinkers you respect.

The coaching reports draw from a curated library of leadership and performance frameworks including:

- **Cal Newport** — Deep Work, digital minimalism, fixed-schedule productivity
- **Greg McKeown** — Essentialism, the disciplined pursuit of less
- **James Clear** — Atomic Habits, systems over goals
- **Ray Dalio** — Principles, radical transparency, pattern recognition
- **Michael Porter** — Competitive strategy, trade-offs, strategic focus
- **Daniel Kahneman** — Thinking Fast and Slow, cognitive bias, decision quality
- **Patrick Lencioni** — Team dysfunction, organisational clarity
- **Peter Drucker** — Effectiveness, time management, knowledge work

Every intervention Cadence gives you is cited. Every recommendation traces back to a framework. That is the difference between advice and coaching.
